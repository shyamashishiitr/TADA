## ðŸ’€ Why Todo Apps Fail

### Common Failure Patterns:

1. **Feature Bloat**
   - Example: Wunderlist â†’ Microsoft killed it, merged into To Do
   - Tried to compete with Asana/Trello
   - Lost core simplicity

2. **No Clear Methodology**
   - GTD? Eisenhower? Kanban? Pomodoro?
   - Apps that support "everything" feel directionless

3. **Sync/Technical Issues**
   - Any.do had major sync problems
   - Lost user trust
   - Data loss = immediate churn

4. **No Differentiation**
   - "Another todo app" syndrome
   - Why switch from what I have?

5. **Pricing Mistakes**
   - Too expensive (loses to free alternatives)
   - Too cheap (can't sustain development)
   - Weird pricing tiers

6. **Platform Lock-in**
   - Things 3 success is Apple-only, but risky
   - Web-only apps lose to native

## ðŸŽ¨ UI/UX Trends (2025-2026)

### What's Working:

1. **Minimal by Default**
   - Hide advanced features
   - Progressive disclosure
   - Linear (project management) is the gold standard

2. **Keyboard-First**
   - Cmd+K everywhere
   - Power users love shortcuts
   - Quick capture is king

3. **Dark Mode**
   - Expected, not optional
   - Midnight modes (dimmer than dark)

4. **Micro-interactions**
   - Satisfying checkmarks
   - Smooth transitions
   - Haptic feedback (mobile)

5. **Brutalist/Neo-Brutalism**
   - Bold colors
   - Thick borders
   - Playful, confident

6. **Glass/Blur Effects**
   - macOS-style transparency
   - Depth through blur
   - Frosted glass cards

### What's Dying:

- Skeuomorphism (checkboxes that look like paper)
- Overwhelming color coding
- Sidebar clutter
- Modal hell
